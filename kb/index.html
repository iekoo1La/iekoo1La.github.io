ECB Advances Digital Euro Development with Updated Rulebook and New Stakeholder Initiatives

Summary:

  • The European Central Bank (ECB) has released its second progress report on the digital euro project, enhancing the rulebook for harmonized digital payments after a review with various stakeholders.
  • The ECB has concluded a call for potential digital euro providers, with selected bidders invited to tender, and results will be released in 2025.
  • New research and engagement efforts are ongoing to understand user preferences, with a focus on vulnerable consumers and small merchants; findings will be available by mid-25.
  • The ECB is collaborating with stakeholders for innovation partnerships to explore conditional payments and other digital euro use cases, with a report set for July 2025.
  • Efforts are underway to develop a method for setting digital euro holding limits, balancing user needs with economic stability; feedback from market participants will inform this process, set for testing in 2025.

Eurosystem Successfully Completes DLT Tests for Central Bank Money Settlement

Summary:

  • The Eurosystem completed over 200 transactions totaling €1.59 billion using Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) from May to November 2024.
  • The tests involved 64 participants1, including central banks, financial market participants, and DLT operators, conducting over 40 trials and experiments
  • Trials included actual central bank money settlement, while experiments used mock settlements.
  • The exploratory tests aimed to understand the opportunities and challenges DLT presents for financial markets and central bank money settlements.
  • In January 2025, participants will meet to discuss findings from the trials, which will help the Eurosystem decide future steps regarding DLT use in settlements.

Norway joins Tips

  • The European Central Bank (ECB) and Norges Bank have signed an agreement
    allowing Norway to join the Eurosystem’s TARGET Instant Payment Settlement
    (TIPS) service.
  • As part of this integration, the Norwegian krone will become the fourth
    currency available for instant payment settlement in TIPS, along with the
    euro, Swedish krona, and soon the Danish krone (due April 2025).
  • The inclusion of the Norwegian krone is scheduled for the first half of 2028, offering Norway’s market participants 24/7 instant payments in central bank
    money.
  • ECB President Christine Lagarde highlighted Norway’s participation as a step towards stronger economic ties across Europe, marking Norway as the first
    non-EU European Economic Area country to join TIPS.
