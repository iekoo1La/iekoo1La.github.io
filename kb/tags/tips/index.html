Norway joins Tips

  • The European Central Bank (ECB) and Norges Bank have signed an agreement
    allowing Norway to join the Eurosystem’s TARGET Instant Payment Settlement
    (TIPS) service.
  • As part of this integration, the Norwegian krone will become the fourth
    currency available for instant payment settlement in TIPS, along with the
    euro, Swedish krona, and soon the Danish krone (due April 2025).
  • The inclusion of the Norwegian krone is scheduled for the first half of 2028, offering Norway’s market participants 24/7 instant payments in central bank
    money.
  • ECB President Christine Lagarde highlighted Norway’s participation as a step towards stronger economic ties across Europe, marking Norway as the first
    non-EU European Economic Area country to join TIPS.

Announcement: TIPS to connect to other fast payment systems globally

  • The European Central Bank (ECB) is advancing its TARGET Instant Payment Settlement service (TIPS) to connect with global fast payment systems to enhance the global payments ecosystem.
  • Initiatives include implementing a cross-currency settlement service allowing exchanges between TIPS and other fast payment systems without direct links.
  • TIPS will join Project Nexus, led by the Bank for International Settlements (BIS), to facilitate instant cross-border payments with selected Asian payment systems.
  • A bilateral link with India’s Unified Payments Interface (UPI) will be established to improve cross-border payments, capitalizing on India’s high transaction volumes and significant remittance inflows.

Annnouncement: TIPS to include cross-currency instant payments service

  • The European Central Bank (ECB) will implement a cross-currency settlement service in the TARGET Instant Payment Settlement (TIPS) framework, allowing instant payments in one currency to settle in another using central bank money.
  • Initial currencies included will be euro, Swedish kronor, and Danish krone, with plans for future expansion to other currencies.
  • The initiative follows exploratory work from 2020 and involves collaboration with Sveriges Riksbank and Danmarks Nationalbank, alongside input from payment service providers (PSPs).
  • This service aims to enhance global payment systems, aligning with the G20 roadmap for efficient payments and supporting the Eurosystem’s strategy for cross-border transactions involving non-euro area partners.
